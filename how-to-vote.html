How to Vote

A simple guide to participating in the election.

Check your eligibility

Ensure you are a registered voter in Nigeria and have your Permanent Voter's Card (PVC). You must be at least 18 years old.

Educate yourself

Review the candidate profiles on this page to make an informed decision. Understand their platforms and what they stand for.

Cast your vote

Navigate to the 'Vote' page, select your chosen candidate, and submit your vote. You will receive a unique transaction ID for verification.

Verify and follow results

Use your transaction ID on the 'Verify Vote' page to confirm your vote was recorded. You can also follow the live results on the 'Results' page.

Meet the candidates

Learn more about the individuals running for office.

Khaled Muhammed Jibril

Unity & Progress Party (UPP)

Khaled Muhammed Jibril is a seasoned administrator with over 20 years of experience in public service. He promises to focus on economic diversification and youth empowerment.

Ngozi Okoro

New Nigeria Alliance (NNA)

Ngozi Okoro is a renowned economist and activist. Her campaign is centered on transparency, anti-corruption, and investing in education and healthcare.

Yakubu Danjuma

People's Democratic Coalition (PDC)

A former military general, Yakubu Danjuma is running on a platform of national security and infrastructure development. He pledges to bring stability and discipline to governance.

Fatima Bello

Social Justice Movement (SJM)

Fatima Bello is a lawyer and human rights advocate. She is dedicated to fighting for social equality, environmental protection, and justice for all Nigerians.
